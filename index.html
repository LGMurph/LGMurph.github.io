I am an observational stellar astrophysicist working as a graduate student at Purdue University with Danny Milisavljevic's time domain astrophysics group. I became an experienced observer with expertise in high-precision, multi-band photometry and echelle spectroscopy by studying interacting binary stars and discovering exoplanets. Currently, I am investigating the rapid follow-up of early supernovae warnings from the SNEWS network, developing an off-campus observatory, and teaching introductory astronomy labs.


Current Research Projects


SNEWS

The SuperNova Early Warning System (SNEWS) is a network of neutrino detectors who send an alert to astronomers when a nearby supernova emits the precursory neutrino burst. I am investigating how to best coordinate the follow-up effort to find the nearby supernova from the localization provided by the neutrino detectors.

PWAO

The Purdue Wildlife Area Observatory (PWAO) is an off-campus astronomical observatory. I am upgrading the equiptment to a 14-inch Celestron Edge HD Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope driven on a Paramount MX+ mount with new camera, filters, and off-axis guider.
