Supernova Early Warning System

The SuperNova Early Warning System (SNEWS) is a world-wide network of neutrino detectors who send an alert to astronomers when a galactic core-collapse supernova emits the precursory neutrino burst. This burst provides a galactic localization on the order of tens of degrees and gives us seconds to hours, depending on the progenitor, to find the star that is undergoing core-collapse.

I am investigating how to best coordinate the ground based follow-up effort to observe the earliest ever seen times of a core-collapse supernova.
